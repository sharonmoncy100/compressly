Compress image for exam forms (JEE, NEET, university portals)

Prepare photos that meet the strict requirements of exam portals. Examples and KB targets for common Indian exam systems.

Exam portals often require a passport-style photo and sometimes a signature image. Requirements may include pixel dimensions, background color and file-size limits. Below are practical steps and common targets used by many Indian exam portals.

Common checks for exam portal uploads

  • File format: JPEG / JPG is accepted almost everywhere. PNG rarely required for photos, but used for signatures with transparency.
  • File size (typical): many portals accept ≤100 KB to 200 KB for photos. Some accept up to 500 KB. Always read the portal guidance.
  • Pixel dimensions: many portals ask for 350 × 450 px, or 200 × 200 px for small thumbnails. When in doubt, 350 × 450 px is a safe starting point.

How to prepare your image

  1. Crop to the required ratio (usually portrait 3:4 or square if requested).
  2. Resize to the portal pixel size (e.g. 350 × 450 px).
  3. Use JPEG output and a target KB (start with 100 KB).
  4. If the portal rejects, slightly lower the target KB or crop tighter and try again.
Practical tip: Keep an unchanged original copy. Create one compressed copy for upload and one slightly larger copy in case the portal requires a higher-quality image later.

FAQ

Q: I uploaded a 100 KB photo and it was rejected. Why?
A: Some portals check pixel dimensions, aspect ratio, or background color as well.

Q: Should signature be PNG or JPG?
A: If the portal requires a transparent signature, use PNG. If transparency is not required, JPG is usually smaller and safe.
