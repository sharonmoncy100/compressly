Users are responsible for ensuring that they have the necessary rights, permissions, or other lawful basis to download, process, or use content accessed through Compressly.

Compressly must not be used to intentionally infringe copyright or other intellectual property rights.

Submitting a copyright complaint

If you are a copyright owner, or are authorized to act on behalf of one, and believe that material accessible through Compressly infringes your copyright, you may send us a copyright infringement notice.

To help us review your request, please include the following information:

  • Your full name and a valid email address or other appropriate contact information.
  • Identification of the copyrighted work that you believe has been infringed.
  • Identification of the specific Compressly URL or functionality associated with your complaint.
  • The URL or other information identifying the material that is the subject of your complaint, where applicable.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the disputed use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or applicable law.
  • A statement that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
  • Your physical or electronic signature.

Where to send a copyright notice

Copyright notices and related legal inquiries can be sent to:

Email: contact@compressly.in

Please use a clear subject line such as "Copyright Infringement Notice" so that we can identify and review your request.

Our response

We may review valid copyright complaints and take appropriate action where necessary. Depending on the circumstances, this may include removing or restricting access to content or functionality associated with a valid complaint.

We may request additional information if a complaint does not contain enough information for us to reasonably identify and evaluate the issue.

Misrepresentation

Please ensure that copyright complaints are accurate and made in good faith. Knowingly submitting false or misleading claims may have legal consequences.

Important: This policy is intended to provide a clear process for reporting copyright concerns. It does not constitute legal advice.
